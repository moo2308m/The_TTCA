Introduction $SHEEPPD(n)$ is a user made computable function, made by me. It's based off of graphs and graph theory and is the second function in the animal series. $SHEEPPD(n)$ equals the maximum length of a finite sequence of distinct straight line graph embeddings, $G_1, G_2, \dots, G_k$, drawn on a flat plane that satisfies the structural growth and coloring limits of the rules. Graph $G_i$ has a limit of $S$ vertices, lines, and colors. Since shapes are undefined in graph theory, a shape is a closed set of lines, with a minimum being 3 lines (a triangle), a shape signature is the unordered multiset of a shape's line colors, and a line is a link connecting 2 points or vertices together. Also, I'll be referencing a function called "$GM(n)$" later. $GM(n)$ means $\text{GraphMinor}(n)$ and has the exact same rules and structure as the $SCG(n)$ function. The equation for $S$ $Z_S = n [\uparrow^{2^{10^{27^n}}}] n + 2^{10^{27}}$ $E_S^1(x) = GM(x [\uparrow^{Z_S}] x)$ For any value of k > 1: $E_S^k(x) = GM(E_S^{(k^x) - 1}(Z_S) [\uparrow^{k + 1}] E_S^{Z_S}(k - 1))$ $S = E_S^{Z_S}(GM(Z_S [\uparrow^{Z_S}] Z_S))$ The rules There are some rules for $SHEEPPD(n)$: 1. $n$ must be a positive integer. 2. Each graph can't have more lines or vertices than the limit, $S$. 3. Inside any graph, lines can't overlap or be inside each other. If a line splits a shape, the outer shape and all smaller shapes made by the split count as valid, individual shapes. 4. A newer graph is illegal if it copies the way an older graph stacks networks of shapes inside each other. If graph 2 has a chain of shapes nested $10^{27}$ levels deep, a younger graph can't contain that hierarchical arrangement of shapes and color signatures anywhere in its system, even if it is stretched, spun around, or buried inside a massive web of lines. 5. The lines aren't like ARGB lights. They can't have multiple colors, and can only be one color at a time.
